Project Overview

UCOST is implementing this SEED-DST funded project targeting the SC/ST community in Uttarakhand's hilly regions — with 18.76% Scheduled Caste and 2.89% Scheduled Tribe populations (Census 2011) — with a comprehensive focus on sustainable livelihood planning, traditional and indigenous knowledge capture, skill upgrades, technology database creation, and geospatial mapping through RS & GIS. SC/ST Cells are being established across all 13 districts of Uttarakhand to coordinate technology adoption for socioeconomic development, scale successful interventions, and systematically build institutional and human capacity. The project bridges the gap between cutting-edge science & technology and the lived realities of hill communities across Garhwal and Kumaon divisions, enabling equitable access to innovation ecosystems, government schemes, and market linkages.

Programme Details

Objectives, Methodology & Deliverables

01 — Livelihood
Assess the livelihood system for sustainable livelihood planning through science, know-how, and practices of SC/ST communities in Uttarakhand's hilly terrain.
02 — Knowledge
Capture traditional and indigenous knowledge and upgrade skills, building on local innovation and knowledge systems unique to SC/ST hill communities.
03 — Database
Establish a comprehensive database of promising technologies for widespread adaptation and replication across all 13 SC/ST districts of Uttarakhand.
04 — Spatial Mapping
Map data and resources on the spatial domain through GIS & Remote Sensing technologies to enable evidence-based policy and programme planning.

The project employs a multi-pronged integrated approach combining food preservation, traditional craft support, indigenous knowledge validation, and geospatial technologies. These four units operate as a connected programme pipeline — each feeding into the overall socioeconomic empowerment of SC/ST communities across all 13 districts of Uttarakhand.

Food Preservation Unit
Horticulture & Agriculture product processing, value addition, and food science interventions
Artisan & Handicrafts Unit
Traditional craft support & market linkage for SC/ST artisans and weavers
Scientific Gazettes Unit
Indigenous knowledge testing, product quality validation, and market linkage documentation
RS & GIS Mapping
Remote Sensing & Geographic Information System spatial resource and community mapping
1
Human Resource Development of SC/ST Community
Comprehensive capacity building and skill enhancement programmes targeting community members across all 13 districts of Uttarakhand.
2
Establishment of Site-based Skill Development Centre
On-ground vocational training and technology transfer centres established within SC/ST habitations for local access.
3
Identification of Gaps in Existing Government / Non-Government Schemes
Evidence-based gap analysis to identify underserved areas, unmet needs, and opportunities for improved policy coverage.
4
Formulation, Strengthening, Coordination & Documentation
Designing and documenting tailored programmes for filling identified gaps through targeted S&T interventions across SC/ST areas.
5
Replicable Model Development through S&T Intervention
Creating scalable and transferable socioeconomic intervention models for SC/ST areas — designed for nationwide replication.
