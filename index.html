Project Overview

UCOST is implementing this SEED-DST funded project targeting the SC/ST community in Uttarakhand's hilly regions — 18.76% SC population and 2.89% ST population (Census 2011) — with focus on sustainable livelihood planning, traditional/indigenous knowledge capture, skill upgrades, technology database creation, and spatial mapping.

SC/ST Cells are being established across all 13 districts of Uttarakhand to coordinate technology adoption for socioeconomic development, scale successful interventions, and build institutional and human capacity.

Objectives, Methodology & Deliverables

1

To assess the livelihood system for sustainable livelihood planning through science, know-how and practices.

2

To capture traditional and indigenous knowledge and upgrade the skill, building on local innovation and local knowledge system.

3

Establishment of database of promising technologies that can be taken up for widespread adaption.

4

Mapping of the above data and resources of spatial domain.

5

To act as coordination cell to promote adaption of appropriate and relevant technologies for socio-economic development of target development.

6

Promotion, replication and scaling up of successful technologies leading to socio-economic employment of capacity building.

7

Development of programs and projects for strengthening human & institutional capacities and technology development.

8

Human Resource development of SC/ST Community.

9

Establishment of site-based skill development centre.

10

Identification of gaps in existing government/non-government schemes.

Site-specific priority areas across Uttarakhand's varying geography, with need-based interventions for socio-economic upliftment via:

Food Preservation Unit

Horticulture & Agriculture Products — processing, value addition, and market linkage for local produce

Artisan & Handicrafts Unit

Skill development, design innovation, and market access for traditional artisans and craftspeople

Scientific Gazettes Preparation Unit

Indigenous/traditional knowledge testing, product quality validation, and market linkage documentation

Mapping through RS & GIS

Spatial mapping of resources using Remote Sensing and Geographical Information System for evidence-based planning

Accessibility Statement

Our Commitment

The Uttarakhand State Council for Science & Technology (UCOST) is committed to ensuring that the SC/ST Cell portal is accessible to all users, including persons with disabilities. The website is designed and maintained to comply with the GIGW 3.0 Guidelines for Indian Government Websites and Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA conformance.

Accessibility Features

  • Skip to Main Content link is provided for quick keyboard navigation.
  • Bilingual support allows switching between English and Hindi content.
  • Font size adjustment controls (A-, A, A+) are available in the top utility bar.
  • High contrast mode switch is available for visually impaired users.
  • Full keyboard accessibility allowing navigation via Tab and arrow keys.
  • Screen reader friendly semantic structure with ARIA landmarks and dynamic announcements.
  • Accessible on-site search engine with keyboard support and screen reader count announcements.

Known Limitations

While we strive to ensure accessibility of all pages, some legacy PDF documents and scanned newspaper clips might not be fully optimized for screen readers yet. We are actively working to replace these with accessible versions.
